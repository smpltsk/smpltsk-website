Why SMPLTSK Exists

The Founder's Story: Living the Problem Daily

"As a registered builder, I deal with tradespeople and sophisticated clients. When a client defect list is created, it must be accurately communicated to the repair team so they can respond efficiently. This is where errors occur. Approximately 65% of the population are visual learners, so I developed SMPLTSK to reduce costly errors by using visual-first communication. The response has been great, with the mental load significantly reduced for clients, workers and myself. Now I also use it for my hobbies and other lifestyle matters to make life simpler. After all, they say a picture is worth a thousand words. Let's work smarter, make things simpler and enjoy a richer life."

Three steps to visual task management

1

Capture

Take a photo of any space, project, or area where tasks need doing. Your room, property, job site, equipment - anywhere.

2

Mark

Drop numbered markers exactly where work needs attention. Add notes if needed. Prioritize visually.

3

Complete

Check off tasks as you finish. Track progress. Share with others. See the transformation happen.
